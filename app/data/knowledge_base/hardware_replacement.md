# Hardware Replacement and Repair Policy

## Overview
This policy covers the process for requesting hardware repairs, replacements, and upgrades for company-issued equipment.

## Covered Equipment
- Laptops and desktops
- Monitors (up to 2 external monitors per employee)
- Keyboards, mice, and docking stations
- Headsets and webcams
- Mobile phones (company-issued only)

## Hardware Lifecycle
- **Standard refresh cycle**: Laptops and desktops are replaced every 4 years.
- **Early replacement**: May be approved if the device has recurring hardware failures or no longer meets job requirements.

## Requesting a Repair
1. **Diagnose**: Try basic troubleshooting first (restart, check cables, run diagnostics).
2. **Submit a ticket**: Go to https://itportal.company.com/hardware and describe the issue.
3. **IT assessment**: A technician will assess the issue within 1 business day.
4. **Repair or replace**: Minor repairs are done on-site. If the device is beyond repair or out of warranty, a replacement will be issued.

## Requesting a Replacement or Upgrade
1. **Submit a ticket**: Describe the current hardware and the business justification for replacement/upgrade.
2. **Manager approval**: Equipment replacements over $500 require manager sign-off.
3. **Provisioning**: IT provisions new hardware within 3-5 business days.
4. **Data migration**: IT will assist with data transfer from old to new device.
5. **Return old equipment**: All old equipment must be returned to IT for secure disposal within 5 business days.

## Loaner Devices
- If your device is being repaired, you may request a loaner laptop from the IT desk.
- Loaner devices must be returned within 2 business days of receiving your repaired/replaced device.

## Lost or Stolen Equipment
- **Report immediately**: Notify IT Security (security@company.com, ext. 4550) and your manager within 24 hours.
- **Remote wipe**: IT will remotely wipe the device to protect corporate data.
- **Police report**: A police report is required for stolen equipment claims.
- **Replacement**: A replacement device will be issued after the incident report is filed.
